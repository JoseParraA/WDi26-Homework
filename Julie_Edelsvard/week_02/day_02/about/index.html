Richard Stallman

Dr. Richard Stallman launched the Free Software Movement in 1983 by announcing the plan to develop the GNU operating system, intended to be composed entirely of free software. He and others began developing GNU in 1984. The last gap in GNU was filled in 1992 when Linus Torvalds liberated his kernel, Linux, resulting in the combined GNU/Linux operating system that is widely used today. In 1985, Dr. Stallman founded the Free Software Foundation and remains its president.
