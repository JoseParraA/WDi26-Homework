What is Perl?

It's a programming language invented by Larry Wall. It's said to be a general-purpose Unix scripting language to make report processing easier. It became populat in the 1990s as a CGI scripting language, one of the reasons for which was its a the time unsurpassed regular expression and string parsing abilities.

The name Perl is apparently a reference to a part of the bible.

It has been said to have a lot of 'line noise' and also to be the 'duct tape of the internet' - being everywhere and being used as a glue language, and also being a bit ugly.

"I'm reminded of the day my daughter came in, looked over my shoulder at some Perl 4 code, and said, 'What is that, swearing?"

Larry discusses the connections between Perl and postmodernism here. He claims that in both postmodernism and Perl, AND is more important than OR.

"In Perl, AND has higher precedence than OR does. There you have it. That proves Perl is a postmodern language.
